Occupational Therapy

Occupational therapists help people of all ages do the things they need and want to do in daily life. Typically, people who are treated by occupational therapists have injuries, disabilities, or illnesses that make it difficult for them to do these day-to-day tasks. Typically, occupational therapists have a degree in something like psychology or communication disorders and get their master’s. For my future career, I am interested in working with disabled kids.

Additional Information

  1. Education Required
    • Bachelor’s degree; preferably in something like communication disorders, psychology, or biology
    • 2-years master’s program
  2. Responsibilities as an Occupational Therapist
    • Assessments and evaluations of the patients
    • Treatment planning and implementation
    • Patient rehabilitation and support
    • Education and advocating for their ability to learn and grow
    • Promoting health and well-being
    • Professional development
  3. Salary Information
    • Typically make $85,000-$100,000 a year
    • Specialized occupational therapists can make over $100,000 a year
